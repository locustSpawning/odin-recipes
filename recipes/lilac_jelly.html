Lilac Jelly

Using flowers for food ~

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups lilac blossoms
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 4 cups sugar
  • 1 box (1.75 oz) pectin

Steps:

  1. Separate 4 cups of lilac blossoms from their stems, carefully removing any green parts.
  2. Pour 4 cups boiling water over the top of the lilac blossoms and allow the tea to infuse for about 10 minutes. It should be a turquoise color (which will change to pink when lemon juice is added).
  3. Strain the lilac tea into a saucepan or jam pot. Add the lemon juice
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil and add the powdered pectin, stirring to dissolve. Allow the mixture to boil for 1 minute before adding sugar. (Note: Do not add the sugar at the same time as the pectin, or before the pectin, or the jell will not set.)
  5. Add the sugar, stirring to dissolve. Bring the mixture back to a full boil for 1 minute before ladling into jelly jars leaving 1/4 inch headspace.
  6. Allow the jars to cool completely on the counter before storing in the refrigerator (for up to a month) or the freezer for up to 6 months.
